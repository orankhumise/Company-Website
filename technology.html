Technology

Leveraging drone and satellite remote sensing technologies to improve mineral exploration, geophysical mapping, and geotechnical monitoring.

Remote Sensing for Modern Mining Projects

Modern mineral exploration and geotechnical monitoring demand accurate information across large areas, often in challenging terrain. Remote sensing provides a practical way to collect and analyse critical data quickly, safely, and cost effectively.

Traditional field-based investigations remain essential, but they can be time-consuming and resource intensive when large areas must be assessed. Drones and satellites allow decision makers to rapidly identify areas of interest and focus field activities where they will have the greatest impact.

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (Drones)

Drones (UAVs) provide a flexible platform for collecting high-resolution geospatial and geophysical information. Flying closer to the ground than conventional aircraft, allows drones to acquire detailed datasets while operating efficiently across rugged and difficult terrain.

UAV Magnetics allows us to rapidly map large areas and identify magnetic anomalies that may be associated with geological structures, lithological boundaries, or mineralisation targets.

Drone-based surveys improve efficiency, reduce field exposure, and provide high-quality data for exploration and engineering projects.

Satellites (Spaceborne Sensors)

Satellite remote sensing enables frequent observation of large regions over extended periods. These datasets provide valuable information about surface conditions, land use changes, geological features, and ground movement.

For mineral exploration, satellite imagery can help identify alteration patterns, structural trends, and other indicators that support target generation and project evaluation.

In geotechnical monitoring, synthetic aperture radar satellites can help detect subtle ground deformation and displacement trends that may indicate developing stability concerns affecting mine tailings facilities, and pit slopes.

The Power of Integration

No technology works on its own. The greatest value comes from integrating multiple datasets with geological and geotechnical expertise.

By combining spaceborne satellite observations with drone surveys, Fieldserve provides a more complete understanding of geological targets, surface conditions, subsurface characteristics, and geotechnical risks than either technology could deliver independently.

This integrated approach allows clients to make better-informed decisions, reduce uncertainty, improve project planning, and focus resources where they can deliver the greatest value.
